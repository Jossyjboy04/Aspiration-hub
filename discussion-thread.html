Sarah, I completely understand what you're going through. During my toughest period last year, I discovered that breaking everything down into tiny, manageable steps was a game-changer. Instead of focusing on the overwhelming big picture, I'd just commit to one small action each day.

Also, connecting with my morning routine became my anchor - even when everything else felt chaotic, those 30 minutes of meditation and journaling gave me a sense of control and clarity.

What helped me was creating a "why" board - a visual reminder of all the reasons I was pushing through the challenges. Photos, quotes, goals written in my own handwriting. When motivation was low, I'd look at it and remember my deeper purpose.

Also, be gentle with yourself. Progress isn't always linear, and that's perfectly okay. üíô

Community support is everything! Don't underestimate the power of sharing your struggles with trusted friends or family. Sometimes just verbalizing what you're going through can lift a huge weight off your shoulders.

I also started a gratitude practice - writing down 3 things I'm grateful for each night. It helped shift my focus from what was going wrong to what was still going right in my life.

Sarah, your vulnerability in sharing this is already a sign of strength! For me, the key was learning to separate what I could control from what I couldn't. I made two lists and focused my energy only on the "controllable" column.

Physical movement also became crucial - even a 10-minute walk could shift my entire mental state. Movement really is medicine for the mind!
